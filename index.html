CRASHCART Principal Network Systems Engineer
  • Designed and deployed a self-contained "hospital-network-in-a-box" to restore critical clinical functionality during ransomware-induced outages, operating fully independent of compromised hospital infrastructure to eliminate co-infection risk during recovery.
  • Architected a hybrid network (Starlink + Cellular Backhaul) with segmented L2/L3 design, point-to-point wireless links, and private CBRS-based cellular LAN, providing resilient, rapidly deployable connectivity under degraded conditions.
  • Reconstructed clinical system dependencies (EHR, lab systems, patient monitoring, radiology/PACS, printing) into a portable architecture; solving hidden middleware and integration constraints required for end-to-end clinical functionality.
  • Engineered critical workflow integrations: remote printing via IPSec VPN tunneling, lab result routing through LIS/middleware stack into EPIC EHR (resource limited), isolated life-critical patient monitoring networks (inherent by vendor design), PACS/DICOM server configuration, and cellular handoff stabilization via PTP master clock (initially restricted by vendor design).
  • Led first live field deployment at El Centro Regional Medical Center, CA and subsequent deployment at ValleyWise Health Medical Center, AZ: identifying real-world constraints (thermal degradation at 110°F, PoE power delivery failure, physical access limitations) and adapting system design to ensure reliable operation under field conditions.
  • Led multi-level research team (undergraduate and graduate students) through concurrent research/engineering sprint cycles: initally scoping workstreams and experiments (under guidance from mentors), eventually managing collaborations, and maintaining delivery quality under high-ambiguity, time-constrained conditions.
